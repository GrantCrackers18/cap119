Cadet Basic Knowledge

Cadet Oath: "I pledge that I will serve faithfully in the Civil Air Patrol Cadet Program, and that I will attend meetings regularly, participate actively in unit activities, obey my officers, wear my uniform properly, and advance my education and training rapidly to prepare myself to be of service to my community, state, and nation."

  • CAP Motto: "Semper Vigilans" (Always Vigilant)
  • Cadet Honor Code: "I will not lie, cheat, or steal, nor tolerate among us anyone who does. Futhermore, I resolve to do my duty and to live honorably, so help me God."
  • Three Missions of CAP: Aerospace Education, Cadet Programs, and Emergency Services (ACE)
  • Core Values of the CAP: Integrity, Volunteer Service, Excellence, and Respect. (IVER)
  • CAP was founded on December 1, 1941.
  • The first National Commander was Maj. Gen. John F. Curry
  • The first Executive Commander was Mr. Gill Rob Wilson
  • The Civil Air Patrol Cadet Program was initiated in October of 1942.
  • The Eight Regions of the Civil Air Patrol: North East, Middle East, Pacific, South West, South East, Great Lakes, Rocky Mountains, and North Central.
  • The Six Wings in the South East Region: Alabama, Florida, Georgia, Mississippi, Puerto Rico, and Tennessee.
  • Definition of Drill: An organized movement of mass.
